What is Smart City ?

  • A Smart City is-
    • An urban system
    • Uses Information & Communication Technology (ICT)
    • Makes infrastructure more interactive, accessible and efficient.
  • Need for Smart City arose due to-
    • Rapidly growing urban population
    • Fast depleting natural resources
    • Changes in environment and climate

    • A smart city is a technologically advanced urban environment that utilizes data-driven solutions to enhance residents' quality of life, optimize urban operations, and address diverse challenges. The term "smart city" denotes the integration of Information and Communication Technologies (ICT), data analytics, and cutting-edge technologies to efficiently manage key city functions such as transportation, energy, healthcare, education, and public safety. Key characteristics include the integration of advanced technologies like sensors, data analytics, artificial intelligence, and the Internet of Things (IoT) to collect and analyze data from various sources. This data-driven approach enables informed decision-making by city officials, optimizing resource allocation and enhancing urban planning. Smart cities prioritize the development of efficient and sustainable urban infrastructure, including intelligent transportation systems, energy-efficient buildings, and innovative waste management solutions. Sustainability is emphasized through the incorporation of renewable energy sources and eco-friendly transportation methods. The overarching goal is to improve residents' quality of life by providing better access to services, efficient public transportation, enhanced safety measures, and increased citizen engagement through technology. Citizen engagement is facilitated through digital platforms, mobile apps, and other technologies that enable feedback and participation in decision-making processes. Smart cities foster innovation and collaboration between public and private sectors, academia, and the community. This collaborative approach contributes to the development and implementation of cutting-edge solutions to urban challenges. Connectivity is fundamental, encompassing widespread access to high-speed internet, integrated communication networks, and a connected ecosystem enabling seamless interactions between devices and systems. Examples of smart city initiatives include intelligent traffic management systems, smart grids for efficient energy distribution, environmental monitoring through sensors, and the use of technology to enhance public safety and emergency response. The dynamic nature of smart cities continually evolves as technology advances, adapting to the unique challenges and priorities of each urban area.
